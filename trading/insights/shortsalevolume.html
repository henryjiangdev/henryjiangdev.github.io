Short Sale Volume


Description: Gather data that showed short volume per company. Then added up all the short volume and divided by total volume of that day. This data set is all short sale volume and total volume of each day.

%Short Sample Mean is 46.39%, and the standard deviation is 2.03%

Total Volume Sample Mean is 4,718,780,080 and the standard deviation is 1,152,885,122.04

Graph

Some interesting points are high volume areas, such as 01/27/2021. Where it peaked sharply. Looking at the crash of 2020. We can see short volume dries up as volume increases, and price falls. VIX was also increasing. The way short volume works is two way, either there is a reduction in shorts entering orders, or there is a reduction of total volume with similar short levels, increasing %Short. With falling %Short and rising volume, we can see that people were buying as the market was dropping. Shorting was stagnant.

After the dip on 03/17/2020, we can see individuals start shorting again, as volume also dries up slowly. Meaning many people were rushing in to short the market when it was rising, expecting further decrease.

Pretty interesting stuff, people probably lost a lot of money trying to short the market after the bottom.
